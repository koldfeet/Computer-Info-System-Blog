INTERNET DURING THE LATE 90s

Businesses started to adapt the internet, but it was more of a play ground for a few people. The internet was more about getting news, sports, and share information with coworkers. Ecom store and money transfer wasn't a thing yet...

Internet in the 90s:

The first time I experience a computer was in 3rd grade around the year 1997. I’m not sure how accurate my memories are, but I do remember playing The Oregon Trail. I am not sure on why we get to play on computers in that class, but it was the best. The Oregon Trail was the best memories I have for elementary school.

Now we are going to fast forward to around the year 2000. I remember my dad took me to Best Buy and bought my first computer. It was a Hewlett-Packard with an Intel platinum 4. I did everything with that computer. It allows me to look up answers for homework, and during the time Google wasn’t a thing yet. I used Ask Jeeves for help on math problems, and Yahoo for everything else. There was also Yahooligan (not sure if they’re still around), it was a Yahoo for kids that have many fun games. I still remember chatting with people who I don’t even know for hours every night to midnight in a game room. During that time, I don’t remember the type and level of toxicity that was on the internet compared to nowadays.

These are my some of my favorite memories growing up when computer and the internet was still young, but the one that takes the cake must be this one. This happened around my freshman year in high school. For elective I chose this computer class, but I can’t remember what it was. All I remember was that we worked with Adobe Flash MX making animation for one semester and the other we use Adobe Dreamweaver to make a website. My first website was a car website that talks about cars and their spec based off Top Gear reviews. All the nerds who like computer always hang out in that room during lunch, and Mr. Miller was perfectly ok with it. What we love to do after we ate lunch, is to sneak into the parking lot to have a cigarette and back to Mr. Miller classroom. All of us talk about the internet, and this one guy name Anthony. He was a punk, rocker, emo, skater kind of kid. He told us about this software called Bear Share. It was one of those software where you can download just about anything. The only problem with this kind of software is that you need to make sure that the music file you want to download is a MP3 file and not and a .exe.

Bear Share showed me a whole new world within the internet. After that, I discovered LimeWire, and it was the same as Bear Share, but they have a much bigger community and a lot more music and software. I told all the guys about LimeWire during lunch, and this other kid told us about this software where you can download that will find product kyes for just about every popular software. Then we have this idea of having Microsoft Office at home so we can do schoolwork.

We think this is how a products key generator works. The user input the company name and software. Then it runs all possible random combinations of every letter on the keyboard until it finds a product key that “might” work. Usually, 1 in every 7 or 12 product keys that the software generated will work. This led to a competition between all of us to see who can download the most music and have the most software. During the time, I don’t think anyone of us thought about how illegal that was. We used the school internet to download Adobe Dreamweaver and Microsoft window ME onto an external hard drive. Then we go home, and farm for product keys. We love the school internet because it was a million times faster than our Net Zero and AOL dial up at home.

Overall, growing up when the internet was young, and innocent was fun. I’ve made long time friends from playing with people in Counter Strike, World of Warcraft, and random people in a chess game chatroom in Yahoo. I also remember my mom friends asking her if our telephone line is broken because of my dial up. It was a whole lot of fun because we were hacking, and we didn’t even know it. To this day, I still wonder if Mr. Miller know what we were doing with the school internet. That was how innocent everything was…

Internet in 2021:

The internet during the late 90s and early 2000s was very important because of school and many other businesses depended on it. The only difference between today and the 90s is that the internet work a lot better and a lot faster. Since it is much faster than the 90s, it is more acceptable by businesses. Companies can lose millions if the internet go downs for a few hours, and most companies won’t be able to operate without the internet at all. I think the economy will slow down and production will not be at this level if it wasn’t for the internet. Many professional educators believe that the internet marks the end of the Industrial Revolution, and kick started the new age of information. The internet is a part of almost everyone’s life, whether it’s for entertainment or for business, and without its society might enter a dark age.
